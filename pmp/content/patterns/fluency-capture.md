## Context

[[Dual Competence]] describes how individual PMs navigate the tension between craft and organizational fluency. Fluency Capture is what happens to the *organization* when that tension is persistently resolved in favor of fluency.

Each promotion cycle marginally favors the more fluent operator. Over time the population shifts — from people who are good at both craft and the org, toward people who are primarily good at the org. As these operators accumulate and gain seniority, they shape the very feedback mechanisms designed to evaluate performance.

## Problem

The feedback mechanisms that should detect divergence between value creation and value capture get captured by the people whose incentive is to defeat them. Metrics theater, narrative management, and optics-over-outcomes shift from edge behaviors to stable strategies. The organization loses its capacity to distinguish real impact from performed impact.

This is distinct from individual drift (see [[Dual Competence]]). Fluency Capture is an organizational condition, not an individual one. The unit of failure is the institution.

## Forces

- **Organizational selection:** each promotion cycle shifts the population toward pure fluency when fluency is rewarded independently of craft
- **Feedback capture:** fluent operators gain influence over the evaluation systems that reward them, creating a self-reinforcing loop
- **Network reinforcement:** those who benefit from performative norms protect each other and the structures that sustain them
- **Invisible degradation:** the shift from real to performed impact is gradual, distributed, and hard to attribute to any individual decision
- **External blindness:** internal reward loops remain intact even as external outcomes degrade — the system feels healthy from the inside

## Navigation Strategies

Unlike individual-level drift, Fluency Capture cannot be corrected from within — the internal correction mechanisms have been compromised. Recovery requires external forcing functions: competitive pressure, leadership change, or crisis. These are not pleasant interventions; they are often the only ones that work.

Structural interventions, where they can be established before capture is complete, include:

- **External calibration:** sustained, unmediated exposure to customers and market outcomes
- **Long-horizon accountability:** outcome ownership over time horizons longer than individual tenure
- **Craft signal reintroduction:** deliberate recalibration of promotion criteria toward product outcomes, not organizational performance

The earlier these are established, the less leverage is required to hold them.

## Consequences

- Genuine craftspeople exit or are progressively marginalized as the environment selects against them
- Internal processes optimize smoothly while external outcomes drift
- Internal coherence and external irrelevance grow in parallel — the organization becomes harder to disrupt from within precisely as it grows more vulnerable from without
- The pattern tends to be invisible until an external shock makes it legible

The organization becomes efficient at perpetuating itself rather than creating external value. This is the dynamic Gall observed at the level of complex systems generally in *Systemantics*: that sufficiently mature systems tend to displace their original purpose with the goal of self-preservation. Fluency Capture is that phenomenon operating inside product organizations.

## Known Uses

- Large enterprise divisions that have grown insulated from market feedback
- Platform or internal-tooling organizations decoupled from end-user outcomes
- Mature product organizations where tenure and political capital substitute for product judgment
- Organizations that have survived long enough for their founding product instincts to be fully replaced by professional management

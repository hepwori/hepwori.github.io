## Context

Product managers in large technology organizations operate within two overlapping systems:

1. **The product system**, where user needs, strategy, and delivery determine success.
2. **The organizational system**, where influence, alignment, and internal navigation determine success.

Both systems reward different behaviors and compound different skills.

## Problem

Advancement and impact depend on excelling within both systems—yet the organization's reward loops disproportionately reinforce the skills of organizational navigation over product craftsmanship. This creates a hidden divergence between what helps someone succeed *inside* the organization and what helps the product succeed *in the world*.

## Forces

- **Feedback velocity:** Organizational rewards (visibility, praise, promotions) occur faster than product outcomes.
- **Attribution clarity:** Success in the org system is legible and credited to individuals; product success is shared and noisy.
- **Portability:** Craft competence travels across contexts; system fluency decays outside its native environment.
- **Career incentives:** Individuals rationally optimize for what is rewarded most predictably.
- **Organizational inertia:** Systems select for behaviors that stabilize themselves.

## Navigation Strategies

Acknowledge dual competence as a real structure of skill, not a moral hierarchy. Develop awareness of which system you are optimizing for at any given time, and at what cost. Recognize that system fluency compounds locally but depreciates on exit; craft competence compounds more slowly but endures.

## Alternative Framing: Value Creation and Capture

Viewed economically, these two competencies correspond to **value creation** and **value capture**.

- **Value Creation** is the act of producing external impact—delivering outcomes for users, customers, or the market. It reflects craft competence: strategy, discovery, prioritization, and execution.
- **Value Capture** is the act of ensuring that the organization recognizes, resources, and sustains that value. It reflects system fluency: securing alignment, sponsorship, visibility, and continued investment.

Healthy organizations connect these two forms of value. Fluency enables capture of the value that craft creates. The danger arises when value capture becomes decoupled from value creation—when people learn to simulate the signals of capture (alignment, optics, metrics theater) without the underlying creation.

In this view, system fluency is not inherently political; it is the translation layer that converts external impact into internal legitimacy. Healthy fluency converts real value into durable institutional support. Unhealthy fluency counterfeits it.

## Consequences

- Individuals may drift toward non-portable strengths that tie them to one organizational ecology.
- Teams risk over-representation of fluent operators and under-representation of product craftspeople.
- The organization may become efficient at perpetuating itself rather than improving its products.

**Degeneration: Fluency Drift.** When system fluency is rewarded independently of intent, the same skills that enable coordination can incentivize manipulation. Over time, the organization's feedback loops reinforce performative alignment and narrative management over genuine impact. The result is an erosion of integrity norms and a shift from influence for progress to influence for optics.

## Known Uses

- Senior PMs in large enterprises who excel in stakeholder orchestration but struggle in smaller or newer settings.
- Startups that hire "big-company PMs" and find their intuition mismatched to low-infrastructure environments.
- Career plateau phenomena where veterans become indispensable locally but irrelevant externally.
